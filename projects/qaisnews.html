The world’s rarest passport is issued by a nation without any land

  • The Sovereign Military Order of Malta – also known as the Knights of Malta – isn’t just a religious Catholic order with nearly 1,000 years of history.
  • It’s also a sovereign nation, with United Nations observer status and its own constitution, but, unusually, without any land.
  • It issues car license plates – without having any roads to drive them on – and its own stamps, currency and passports.
